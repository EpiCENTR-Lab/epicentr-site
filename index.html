Epithelial Cell Biology in ENT Research

The EpiCENTR group studies how airway epithelial stem cells build, maintain, and repair the respiratory epithelium.

Research

Stem Cell Biology and Disease Modelling

We study how airway epithelial stem cells establish, maintain, and repair the respiratory epithelium. Our work focuses on the molecular mechanisms that regulate stem cell behaviour, including interactions with the extracellular matrix and how these go wrong in epithelial diseases.

We have developed lentiviral tools to study airway differentiation and identified Wnt signalling as a key regulator of basal cell proliferation.

Cell and Gene Therapy

We investigate translational approaches to repair or replace damaged airway tissue using cell- and gene-based therapies. This includes exploring how epithelial cells can be expanded, engineered, and delivered safely and effectively for regenerative applications.

We have developed a new method to expand epithelial basal cells ex vivo . As an initial application, we have focused on epidermolysis bullosa, where we demonstrated proof-of-concept for a combined cell and gene therapy approach.
